How to Spot and Support a Friend in Need

Mental health challenges like depression can be difficult to see, but small changes in behavior or mood can be important signals. Recognizing them early and offering support can make a real difference.

Spotting the Signs

Look for persistent sadness, withdrawal from social activities, changes in sleep or appetite, loss of interest in hobbies, or sudden irritability. Trust your instincts if something feels “off.”

How to Help

Start with kindness and patience. Listen without judgment, avoid minimizing their feelings, and remind them they don’t have to go through it alone. Sometimes just being present is the best support.

What to Do Next

Encourage professional help from a doctor or therapist. Offer to help them find resources or go with them to appointments. If they are in crisis or talk about self-harm, call emergency services right away.

Remember: reaching out to a friend can change their path. Compassion and action go hand in hand.
